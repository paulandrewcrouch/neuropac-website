Accessibility Statement

Last updated: September 2025

Our commitment

NEUROPAC LIMITED is committed to making our website usable by everyone. We aim to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines wherever possible.

Measures we take

  • Use of semantic HTML landmarks (header, main, footer).
  • Keyboard focus visibility and logical tab order.
  • Responsive layout that adapts to different screen sizes and orientations.
  • Colour contrast designed to meet WCAG AA for text and interface elements.
  • Text resizing supported (200%+) without loss of content or functionality.
  • Descriptive alternative text for meaningful images.

Compatibility

This site is designed to work with current versions of major browsers, including Safari (iOS/iPadOS), Chrome, Edge and Firefox.

Known limitations

We’re working to improve the following:

  • Some third-party content or links may not fully meet WCAG criteria.
  • Legacy documents (e.g., older PDFs) may not be fully accessible.

Feedback and contact

If you face any accessibility barriers or need information in a different format (e.g., large print, audio description), please contact:
team@neuropac.co.uk

Enforcement

If you contact us and are not satisfied with our response, you can seek advice from Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS).

Assessment approach

We self-assess this website’s accessibility against WCAG 2.1 AA and periodically review templates and components when we update the site.

Preparation of this statement

This statement was prepared in September 2025 and will be reviewed at least annually or whenever significant changes are made to the website.
